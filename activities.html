AROS

I spent a day exploring the Aros Art Museum, an institution known for contemporary art and the biggest art gallery in Aarhus. During my visit, one floor in particular I really like was the Erró exhibition - 'The Power of Images'. The exhibition showcased so many different styles of artworks by the artist, showcasing distinctive style that merges pop culture, history, and vibrant color palettes. Among the museum's various attractions, the rainbow tunnel stood out as another personal favourite. On the top floor of this builing stands a is a circular walkway spanning 150 meters in length and three meters in width. Constructed from glass panels displaying a full spectrum of rainbow colors, the tunnel offers panoramic 360º vistas overlooking both the city of Aarhus and its picturesque bay. I enjoyed ending my visit seeing the view of Aarhus and enjoying the colours all around.

Gender Museum

The Aarhus Gender Museum is a unique institution dedicated to exploring and challenging societal perceptions of gender. Through its exhibits, events, and educational initiatives, the museum aims to promote understanding, dialogue, and awareness about gender identity, roles, and experiences. Gender expression and idenity are important issues to me and it was ver important for me to attend this museum during my stay. I found many parts of this museum interesting and found the yellow wall comparing gendered products eye-opening as there were more products than I had anticipated. I also enjoyed the exhibition looking at the evolution of bags and how they have been used by different genders and for different purposes overtime.

Kunsthal Aarhus

Kunsthal Aarhus is a contemporary art center which serves as a prominent cultural hub, dedicated to showcasing a diverse range of contemporary art exhibitions, projects, and events. The institution provides a platform for both emerging and established artists, offering them opportunities to share their work and engage with the public. During my visit there were two exhibtions. The first was Nathalie Du Pasquier 'paintings of things. paintings as objects' I loved how vibrant and colourful all her work was and I found her sculptures incredibly interesting alongside her paintings of similar themes. The second exhibition was Simon Dybbroe Møller with 'Hypnic Jerk.' The centerpiece is the captivating video projection "Bag of Bones," featuring a digital animation of the Grauballemand, a renowned Iron Age man. This work exemplifies Dybbroe Møller's artistic process, delving into the evolution of the Grauballemand's meaning over time. I found this exhition a lot more intense, however, I had never experienced art similar to his style so I was incredibly intrigued and liked his work overall.

Mosegaard Museum

The Moesgaard Museum, is a renowned cultural institution that offers visitors a rich and immersive experience delving into various aspects of human history and cultural heritage. The museum is situated in a striking modern building surrounded by lush green landscapes, creating a harmonious blend of architecture and nature. The museum's collection encompasses a diverse array of artifacts, including archaeological findings, ethnographic objects, and art pieces, all curated to provide a comprehensive understanding of different cultures and eras. I love history and I was able to learn a lot of new information around the Vikings, Stone Age, Iron Age and more. Additionally, I was able to leanr more about the Grauballemand as mentioned previously which enabled be further to undersatnd the work of Dybbroe Møller. I had never heard of this preserved man before this trip and the museuum put a lot of emphasis as to why it was such a groundbreaking discovery which was incredibly fascinating.

City Hall

Aarhus City Hall, located in Aarhus, Denmark, stands as an iconic landmark that not only serves as a municipal administration center but also boasts an impressive artistic treasure within its walls – the famous City Hall Mural. Designed by renowned Danish artist Asger Jorn, this mural is a captivating depiction of abstract art that adds an artistic dimension to the city's administrative hub. Our tour guide explained what each element of this mural means which was very interesting to me as it explained Danish culture well and a lot of time went into this mural. The view at the top of the city hall was beautiful. It was a gorgeous day and I was able to see all of Aarhus from a different persepective.
